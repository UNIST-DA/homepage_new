- **Imputation** is a technique for valid statistical inference with incomplete data, not just for filling in values.
- **Maximum Likelihood Estimation (MLE)** provides unbiased parameter estimates directly from incomplete data under the MAR assumption.
- The **EM algorithm** is a practical method to find MLE estimates for complex missing data patterns by iteratively updating statistical information.
- **Multiple Imputation (MI)** generates multiple complete datasets to reflect the uncertainty of missing values, allowing for the calculation of valid standard errors.
- **Missing Not at Random (MNAR)** situations can be analyzed using the **Selection Model** or the **Pattern-Mixture Model**.
